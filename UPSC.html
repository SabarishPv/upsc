Understanding the Examination

A crystal-clear understanding of the exam structure is the first step. The UPSC CSE is a three-stage process:

  1. Preliminary Examination (Prelims): An objective screening test.
    • General Studies Paper I: Evaluates general knowledge.
    • CSAT: A qualifying paper testing aptitude (min 33% required).
  2. Main Examination (Mains): A descriptive written exam.
    • Nine papers in total, including Essay, GS I-IV, and two Optional subject papers.
  3. Personality Test (Interview): The final stage assessing suitability for a career in civil services.

The Do's and Don'ts of Preparation

Discipline is paramount. Follow these guidelines to stay on track.

Do's Don'ts
Analyze the syllabus & past papers. Don't start preparation blindly.
Build a strong foundation with NCERTs. Don't jump to advanced books first.
Read a national newspaper daily. Don't neglect current affairs.
Make concise notes for revision. Don't hoard study material.
Practice answer writing consistently. Don't wait until after Prelims to start.
Maintain a healthy and balanced lifestyle. Don't burn yourself out.

Crafting a Winning Routine

A balanced, realistic schedule is your best friend. Adapt this sample to your needs.

Sample Daily Plan:

  • Morning (6 AM - 12 PM): Physical activity, newspaper reading, and first major study session (e.g., Polity/History).
  • Afternoon (12 PM - 6 PM): Second study session (e.g., Economy), optional subject, and answer writing practice.
  • Evening (6 PM - 10 PM): Daily revision, light reading, and planning for the next day.

Weekly Plan:

  • Mon-Fri: Stick to the daily schedule and targets.
  • Saturday: Weekly revision and one full-length mock test.
  • Sunday: Analyze the mock test, plan for the next week, and take some time to relax and recharge.

Essential Resources & Study Material

Quality over quantity. Stick to a limited set of high-quality resources.

  • Foundation: NCERTs (Class 6-12) for all relevant subjects.
  • Polity: 'Indian Polity' by M. Laxmikanth.
  • History: Spectrum's 'A Brief History of Modern India' & old NCERTs.
  • Economy: Indian Economy by Ramesh Singh & the annual Economic Survey.
  • Environment: Shankar IAS Academy's book.
  • Current Affairs: The Hindu or The Indian Express, plus a monthly magazine.
